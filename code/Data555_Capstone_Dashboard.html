Why This Dashboard Matters: Poor nutrition — including high rates of anaemia among women and undernutrition among children — remains a critical public health challenge in Nepal, and understanding how women's empowerment and land ownership shape dietary diversity is essential to designing effective interventions. This dashboard enables FNGAP program staff and researchers to interactively explore structural determinants of nutrition across Siraha, Saptari, and Udaypur, supporting evidence-based decisions to improve food security for beneficiary families.

About the Project & Data

Women's Empowerment and Nutrition — FNGAP Nepal Outcome Evaluation

Data Source FNGAP Nepal Outcome Evaluation Survey, implemented by CARE Nepal and the National Farmers Group Federation (NFGF) in Madhesh Province, Nepal.
Sample Size 525 FNGAP beneficiary households via multistage systematic random sampling across 10 municipalities; after excluding male respondents and one age outlier, 516 women were included in analyses.
Data Collection Cross-sectional household survey. Dietary diversity was measured using three indicators: A7 (plant-based food consumption), A9 (animal-sourced food consumption), and a composite A7+A9 measure.
Study Population Women in FNGAP beneficiary households. Women's empowerment (Score A) and household land ownership are the primary exposures; dietary diversity is the outcome of interest.
Location Siraha, Saptari, and Udaypur districts, Madhesh Province, Nepal.
Analysis Crude and adjusted logistic regression models assessed associations between land ownership / empowerment score and dietary diversity outcomes, adjusting for primary occupation, district, and household size. Conducted in R.
Confidentiality This dataset is confidential and not publicly available. Source data are not included in this repository.

Dashboard developed by Mia Yang — Department of Epidemiology, Rollins School of Public Health, Emory University. FNGAP / CARE Nepal / NFGF.
